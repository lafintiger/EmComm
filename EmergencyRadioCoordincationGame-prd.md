# **Emergency Radio Coordination Game ‚Äì PRD and Training Design**

## **1. Overview**

**Emergency Radio Coordination** is a live-action communication and coordination game designed to teach participants how to operate under pressure, prioritize resources, and communicate effectively using radio systems.
It is part disaster simulation, part strategy challenge, and part teamwork exercise.

Players act as citizens, responders, and specialists in a community recovering from a crisis where communication networks are partially down.
The only way to find people, deliver aid, and coordinate support is through radio contact and smart decision-making.

This activity trains:

* Radio communication discipline
* Emergency coordination and prioritization
* Situational awareness and decision timing
* Team adaptability and leadership

It‚Äôs built to be scalable ‚Äî ideal for families, classrooms, CERT teams, or full community drills.

---

## **2. Learning Goals**

By the end of play, participants should:

1. **Understand basic radio communication protocols** (call signs, message brevity, over/clear, priorities).
2. **Demonstrate resource coordination under time pressure.**
3. **Apply situational awareness** to manage priorities and movement.
4. **Reflect on communication accuracy and decision quality** during debrief.

The game aligns with FEMA‚Äôs **ICS communication concepts**, CERT coordination principles, and **ARRL emergency operation protocols**.

## **3. Scenario Context**

### **Base Scenario**

A major storm has knocked out parts of the power grid and disrupted cell networks.
People are stranded in different areas ‚Äî each with something someone else needs.
The goal: **Find who you‚Äôre looking for and get needed supplies delivered safely.**

## **4. Gameplay Overview**

### **Setup**

* 3 primary locations (can be three rooms, outdoor zones, or distant landmarks ~100 yards apart).
* Each location starts with 2+ players.
* Each player receives a **Role Card** containing:

  1. Who they are (name, profession)
  2. Who they are looking for
  3. What they have
  4. What they need
  5. Their starting location number (1, 2, or 3)

### **Core Objective**

* **Find your target** (person you‚Äôre looking for).
* **Deliver or obtain** your needed item.
* **Coordinate movements and priorities** through radio communication.

### **Win Condition**

When:

* All players have connected with the people they‚Äôre seeking, and
* All ‚Äúneeds‚Äù have been met through proper transfers.

Time and efficiency are scored.

## **9. Game Mechanics**

### **Movement**

* Movement is **physical** between locations.
* Players decide whether to move or stay each round.
* If two people move toward each other simultaneously, they may **pass each other** ‚Äî causing delays.

### **Timing**

* Each round = 10‚Äì15 minutes (adjustable).
* Facilitators can use a visible timer or bell for turn changes.
* Total session typically runs **45‚Äì90 minutes**.

### **Priority Rules**

* Some players (like doctors or mechanics) may be needed by more than one person.
* Organizers assign **priority flags** for time-sensitive needs (e.g., ‚ÄúMedical in 10 min or fail condition‚Äù).
